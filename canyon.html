Wild Canyon

Adrenaline Combo

Overview Transportation Similar Tours FAQs Contact

Description

If you’re up for an adrenaline-packed day, the Wild Canyon Adrenaline Combo is a blast! It kicks off with 8 zip lines soaring across a canyon—breathtaking views and pure excitement. Feeling extra daring? There’s an optional rappel and a 45-foot climbing wall to test your skills.

Then comes the best part—rafting or tubing down a Class 2-3 river. It’s nonstop fun as you ride the rapids, splash around, and maybe even soak your friends. After all that action, you’ll refuel with a delicious buffet lunch.

To end the day, you’ll cool off with a swim at a stunning waterfall like Llanos de Cortes or Oropendola. An epic mix of thrills, adventure, and unforgettable moments!

What's Included

  • River Tubing on Class 2-3 river
  • Petroglyphs along the Canyon
  • Swimming Spot inside the Canyon
  • Zip-Lining Tour (+ optional rappel/climbing section)
  • Lunch (Buffet Style)
  • Transportation (pick up & drop off)

Frequently asked questions

What should I wear?

Comfortable clothes are a must—something you can move around in for the zip-lining and rafting. Don’t forget your swimsuit, water shoes, and a towel for the waterfall swim at the end of the day.

How long does the tour last?

Including the 1-hour and 20-minute drive each way, it’s a full-day adventure packed with excitement. Be ready for a full day of fun and exploration!

Do I need prior experience for the zip-lining, rappelling, or rafting?

Not at all! The guides will provide you with all the necessary instructions and gear. They’re there to make sure you have a safe and thrilling experience, even if it’s your first time.

What if I don’t want to rappel or climb?

No problem! The rappel and climbing wall are completely optional. If you prefer to skip it, you can just enjoy the zip-lining and rafting without missing out on the fun.

Is rafting and tubing safe?

Absolutely. The river is Class 2-3, which means it’s exciting without being too extreme. The guides are experienced and will ensure your safety throughout the adventure.

What kind of food is included in the lunch buffet?

The buffet offers a variety of delicious options, perfect for refueling after your adventures. From fresh local dishes to satisfying comfort food, there’s something for everyone to enjoy.

What should I bring for the waterfall swim?

Pack a swimsuit, water shoes, and a towel for the waterfall swim. The cool, refreshing water is the perfect way to wrap up the day.

Is this a private tour?

Yes! This is a private tour, so you’ll have the experience all to yourself (with a minimum of 3 people in your group), making it even more special and personalized.

What if it rains?

Rain is common in Costa Rica, and a little drizzle won’t stop the fun. Most activities continue as planned, but if the weather becomes unsafe, the tour will be adjusted, or activities will be rescheduled. Safety is always the top priority.

What is the cancellation policy?

Since we’re not the direct tour operators, we recommend contacting the company directly for any cancellation or refund policies. We’ll help connect you with them to ensure everything is handled smoothly.
